In the Orchard

The cold white burn of stars
flecks the sky like sugar,
the cold white softness
soft as a blanket.

The white earth of the grave.

White sheets on the bed.

White snow in the cornfield.

A dark and tattered tree
by a crystalline brook
that's laughing over the stones.

Autumn cries with its trees
and shakes in sobbing gusts.

The leaves rustle in the wind
like a sleeper shifting in bed.

Dressed in mourning black
I walk through the orchard
among the heavy gusts of leaves
in November's orange light,
which is like a warm apple cider,
waiting for the apples to bloom,
to blossom out of nothing,
like a scream.


The Remnants of Autumn

Autumn corn of autumn leaves
in orange and red and yellow.

Vernal leaves drift in the wind
like white icebergs on the sea.

Dressed, as always, in black,
I walk through this storm of leaves
until the soft warm rain subsides
and I find a clear little waterfall
formed of crooked silver-grey rock,
like a moon directing the warm waters.

The moon shines
over a black stretch of forests
and snow white as the moon.

The bark is black
as with a burning
and caked with snow
like white gauze.

Burned by the fiery colors of autumn,
the fields are ablaze with indian corn.


Many Darknesses

A dark street curves upward
through the night,
drenched in loneliness.

Peach-colored streetlamps
burn alone in the emptiness.

A yellow candle burns
behind the dark pane
of a window.

I put my palm to the cool glass
then rest my head there as if to sleep
and now I sit with my head resting in my hand,
weeping, at the brown table
upon which the candle sits.

A candle burns
in a red glass
bloodying a bedroom
with a doleful light.

I stare at the red sunset on the horizon
through a sliding glass door
over the shapes of black-green conifers.

Night falls deep and blue.

A black chandelier
is dimmed to eerie embers.

Darkness waits in an open door.
The door slams closed on its own.

An old wooden gate
swings back and forth
for no reason.

A pale blue flash of lightning
reveals a child
standing in the middle of the night
clutching a doll.

She has pale skin
and she stares at me
with her black irises.

At that moment, everything ends.
