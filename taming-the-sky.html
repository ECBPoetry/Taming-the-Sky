Foundlings

The night is dark
and there is nothing in the sky.

There is a cold in the heights.
A frozen tarn
lays impassive on the peak
and a brutal wind does sweep
across the meadows,
crackling among the broken snow.

Flames shiver in a ring of stone
and a foundling trembles
in a quaking dance,
hearkening to the freezing chant
of the night's syllables of snow
that not so much resemble
as suggest the clouds
smeared across the blue tableau
some hours ago
before he reached out and stole
those sparks from the night,
before the stars grew unwilling,
when he could still see
those pale inkblots in the sky.


The Hobbyhorse

Scrawled
upon the dust,
in the dead hand
of a broken toy,
the messages stand.

Sprawled,
splintered and disjoined,
disassembled they creak,
sawing the floor beneath.
They speak
of a drought in the garden
and a grinding of teeth.

The salt of the sea
has risen and fallen,
burning out in the sun,
sweeping past
where a river did run,
where cowboys are riding fast,
firing their wooden guns.


The Crossing

The sky's net of stars
is cast across the night
into a thirsty sea of eyes.
The bars
of light slide
through the dark
as the wind rattles
a closing note.

The sun shatters
in the sea's engulfing throat,
the result
of unstoppable decline.

The sea's lumbering strides
propel it to the mark
and it lifts a stealthy hand
to scribble in a cursive pen
on the disappearing land.

The distance
between coasts
grows thin.

Foaming ghosts
breach the ocean.


Cage's Lost Symphony

A mumble
rolls through a blank room,
steeped in the wine that
sloshes in glass wounds,
and from the rubble
of a mouth
a light laugh
erupts.

The sky holds a smoking gun
and a fog grips the fence,
its fingers twisted around
the chain's metal throat
in a rattling ascent.
Drops of rust fleck the street
and a machine
screeches its final note.


Unearthed

A beam flails about
in the dark.
A stark
and empty scene
of earth and timber
surrounds
him. He sees
the theater's
barren stage
upon which this drama will rage
and he laughs,
choking on the dust
of a thousand magic tricks,
a thousand illusions,
a thousand dreams
of something beyond this place
whose tiny walls
have left their mark on his face.

He lets loose one
echoing call
and hears his audience scream
as he taps
his wooden stick.

The rust
of unending nights
explodes with a crack
and somewhere a clap
resounds beyond the sweeping light.


Tenders of the Garden

Roses explode in the storm
as the wind whips
the bloodless garden
into a hazard of thorns.

Gray clouds fulminate,
convulsing the sky,
winds flailing
as the skin is ripped.

Lightning veins the night,
throbbing in darkness,
as the rain pulses.
Its falling seeds germinate
in the wailing
of the wind.

Slipping on the wet stone,
they cut through the brush,
seeking a feeble shelter.

From behind the hedgerow
the moon chances a look
and the night trembles.


Spindrift

The moon falls
like a silver hook,
sinking through the night,
exploding on the surface
and falling back
in limp strings of light.

A line is carelessly cast
in the sea's blue scrawl.
The tool bends its gentle crook
and turns its
eye upon itself.

The vessel groans and shifts,
restless in the waves,
as the line is sent,
grasping, into the dark.

Far below something awaits
and the line jumps with a start.
The sea whitens
in the moon's mad grip
and a man shivers on his boat.
The sea's grip tightens
and he disappears down its throat.


The Beginning of Night

Words are throbbing in the light.
Grinding gears are shaking,
rattling in the wind.
They're humming the lines
the keys are playing
as they chatter
in the cold.

Fog is billowing
as the light changes
and the fool dances
before his mic.

The light shatters
in its strobing,
the bits
falling in the crowd.
In the front row
a zealot rages,
grasping after the damaged
and twisted lights,
while, from the stage,
a laughter sounds
at this ending of days.


A Turning of Keys

Bars of light are sliding
through the trees.
Leaves are rippling
on the branch
like the turning of keys.

A car is writing
a tale of roads
in long, lazy curves.

Autumn bursts like a match,
sets the trees smoldering,
ash falling as the leaves burn.
The wind blows
a few puffs of smoke
into the last bit of sky.

A candle is sitting,
flickering,
in an orange sun,
smiling at the streets below.

The moon rides into the night.
You can almost hear those engines hum.


Broken

Clouds bubbled in the sky
as sunlight poured down
into the clear day.

Light laughter
like glasses arose
as teeth clinked.

In the sunlight
eyes rapidly blinked,
devouring the glare.

The weird play
of dancing trash
stumbled down the road
in a gust of air.

A face shattered,
wide open,
with a crack
and any bonds or vows
were neatly broken.


Turning On

The earth turns
its eye to the sun
as it spins its way
through the night.

A dervish's trail
unfurls
in sandy lines
and candles wink
at the sight.

A puff
of dust whirls
through the temple
of the night
and settles
in blinking signs
and eyefuls of sleep.


Ex Nihilo

From the dark abyss
a line
of light
erupts.
In the simple
hunger of emptiness
it grows serpentine,
bending itself,
coiling,
the glint
of stars
exploding on its back.

A spring lunges,
dark earth bursting apart
as it swells.

The needless
blossom is cracked.


The Patient Sky

The flower opens its eye,
lids unfurling,
and the fruit of the earth
drips onto the soil.

The liberation of decay
disintegrates its rind,
like great towers turning
to sand in the unending surge
of the patient sky,
with the thankless toil
and unceasing work
of bitter days.

The nights are growing festive
and the winter's flurries deep.

Ships are growing restless
as they bob in the distilling seas.

The winds are taken to screaming...
and to the sowing of seeds.


Passing Through

The night
swings its bat
at an orange sun
and it shatters
into twilight.
Seeds of light
are scattered
across the sky
as the darkness runs.

Behind its mask
a ghost pursues
the night
as the winds
pass through
the dark.

Torn from their husk
the stars fly free,
hurtling through space.

The ghost caresses its new face
and, though by night winds unmoved,
the garb
it wears is unwinding
like the fading of the mist
in the risen sun.


A Quiet Song

The sky waves
its banner,
it whips in the wind,
and light rain
sounds a march
across the damp earth.

With a spark
the storm's rapid fuse
is ablaze.
The daylight explodes
with a crash,
metal contorting
through the sky.

In the haze
twisted wreckage looms
and settles
in a slow collapse.

The storm seems
to give up,
loosen its tight grasp
on the rain
and the light.
The sound of its drums
has run thin.

A white flag
ripples in the sky
and is gone
leaving just the wind
blowing through
the clear blue
with its quiet song.


Lasting But a Moment...

The sun has fallen
and the day's funeral
has begun,
lowering its coffin
into the earth. The
sky grows dark
with mourning.

Wine sloshes into place
before the glasses have risen
in their toasting,
flashing with dull sparks
in the flickering light.

The crickets are singing
in the tittering night
and the stars do shake
with laughter.

Lights are winking
in the hereafter.


Dimly Remembered

A shattered green vine
spilling its wine.
I eat the shards,
use them to cut out my heart.

I hold it aloft before the gods.
It beats its wings like a dove.


A Sound Like Sleep

The wind shakes the trees in sobbing gusts.
The starlight shatters into falling dust.
A sound like a sleeper shifting in bed
rises. This thread
unravels.

An untangled web is loosed and travels,
skittering over the concrete,
sweeping once lost streets
until they're emptied.

Dead seeds
awaken and burst.
A mad thirst
trembles in the throat
that only knows this one note,
singing its malison.

Maybe I'll sing along, just this once.


Angle

So casual in that red leather chair,
the smoke scatters with a tortured air
as he taps his cigar,
the ash falls like a thick scar
sliding from a glowing wound.

A stillness in this seething room...
He makes a gesture, a ripple of his hand.
What to make of this man?

Something collapses beyond the wall.

I can feel the crawl
of my hands as they spider
along the wood. Tighter
and tighter, they strangle
the place I sit. An angle

I throw between us
could be what
saves me in the end
but where will I be then?


The Fall

Something was aflutter,
wings beaten by the gutter,
crumpled and thrown away.

I saw you flailing in the wind one day,
whispering when you touched the ground,
and I thought to myself, what now?

I could barely make you out
over this new sound
and you were dirty in your
tarantella, smudged and impure.

You had sold all that was left
and you were twisted into a dress
that was far too small.

Oh, how the mighty do fall.


In the Orchard

The cold white burn of stars
flecks the sky like sugar,
the cold white softness
soft as a blanket.

The white earth of the grave.

White sheets on the bed.

White snow in the cornfield.

A dark and tattered tree
by a crystalline brook
that's laughing over the stones.

Autumn cries with its trees
and shakes in sobbing gusts.

The leaves rustle in the wind
like a sleeper shifting in bed.

Dressed in mourning black
I walk through the orchard
among the heavy gusts of leaves
in November's orange light,
which is like a warm apple cider,
waiting for the apples to bloom,
to blossom out of nothing,
like a scream.


The Remnants of Autumn

Autumn corn of autumn leaves
in orange and red and yellow.

Vernal leaves drift in the wind
like white icebergs on the sea.

Dressed, as always, in black,
I walk through this storm of leaves
until the soft warm rain subsides
and I find a clear little waterfall
formed of crooked silver-grey rock,
like a moon directing the warm waters.

The moon shines
over a black stretch of forests
and snow white as the moon.

The bark is black
as with a burning
and caked with snow
like white gauze.

Burned by the fiery colors of autumn,
the fields are ablaze with indian corn.


Many Darknesses

A dark street curves upward
through the night,
drenched in loneliness.

Peach-colored streetlamps
burn alone in the emptiness.

A yellow candle burns
behind the dark pane
of a window.

I put my palm to the cool glass
then rest my head there as if to sleep
and now I sit with my head resting in my hand,
weeping, at the brown table
upon which the candle sits.

A candle burns
in a red glass
bloodying a bedroom
with a doleful light.

I stare at the red sunset on the horizon
through a sliding glass door
over the shapes of black-green conifers.

Night falls deep and blue.

A black chandelier
is dimmed to eerie embers.

Darkness waits in an open door.
The door slams closed on its own.

An old wooden gate
swings back and forth
for no reason.

A pale blue flash of lightning
reveals a child
standing in the middle of the night
clutching a doll.

She has pale skin
and she stares at me
with her black irises.

At that moment, everything ends.


Insensate

An arching black-streaked gate
protects a solid darkness.

Massive dark tree trunks
huddle together,
whispering.

Water drips
from the stalagtites
onto dark
subterranean pools.

Someone stands silhouetted
in the entrance to the cave.

He's staring at me
through slits of light.

A torch burns
in a sconce
with a crayon fire.

Up ahead the path divides,
each choice unappealing.

A square of light
floats through
an abandoned room.

Gossamer curtains
move in an unfelt wind.

Insensate.
