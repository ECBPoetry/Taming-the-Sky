I




Foundlings

The night is dark
and there is nothing in the sky.

There is a cold in the heights.
A frozen tarn
lays impassive on the peak
and a brutal wind does sweep
across the meadows,
crackling among the broken snow.

Flames shiver in a ring of stone
and a foundling trembles
in a quaking dance,
hearkening to the freezing chant
of the night's syllables of snow
that not so much resemble
as suggest the clouds
smeared across the blue tableau
some hours ago
before he reached out and stole
those sparks from the night,
before the stars grew unwilling,
when he could still see
those pale inkblots in the sky.


The Hobbyhorse

The bones of the beast
have cracked and split,
breaking apart in the line,
its inertia running out
in the petrified pines,
as the teeth of drought
are bleaching into white.

The salt of the sea
has risen and fallen,
burning out in the sun,
the sand sweeping past
where a river did run,
where cowboys are riding fast,
firing their wooden guns.



The Crossing

Cold white bars
of light slide
through the dark
as the wind rattles
a closing note.

The sun shatters
in the sea's engulfing throat,
the result
of unstoppable decline.

The sea's lumbering strides
propel it to the mark
and it lifts a stealthy hand
to scribble in a cursive pen
on the disappearing land.

The distance
between coasts
grows thin.

Foaming ghosts
breach the ocean.


Cage's Lost Symphony

The room is a white hand
holding a bird of smoke
whose body billows forth
in the sharp staccato
of a vibrating laugh.
Drops of wine pound
below the beat of forks
on fine china drums.

The sky holds a smoking gun
and a fog grips the fence,
its fingers twisted around
the chain's metal throat
in a rattling ascent.
Drops of rust fleck the street
and somewhere a dying machine
screeches its final note.


Unearthed

His flashlight waves
its wand of light
above the crowd of pine
that creaks in applause
at his every step
on the dusty stage.

The emptiness thrusts
its swords of dark
through illusion's heart
as a curtain drops
inside his chest
before a crowd of blood.

Slowly the umbra
of a burning wick
draws with its shade,
and now a stain
creeps along
the empty canvas.

Suddenly the rust
of unending nights
explodes with a crack,
and somewhere a clap
resounds beyond
the sweeping light.


Tenders of the Garden

Roses explode in the storm
as the wind whips
the bloodless garden
into a hazard of thorns.

Gray clouds fulminate,
convulsing the sky,
winds flailing
as the skin is ripped.

Lightning veins the night,
throbbing in darkness,
as the rain pulses.
Its falling seeds germinate
in the wailing
of the wind.

Slipping on the wet stone,
they cut through the brush,
seeking a feeble shelter.

From behind the hedgerow
the moon chances a look
and the night trembles.


Spindrift

The moon falls
like a silver hook,
sinking through the night,
exploding on the surface
and falling back
in limp strings of light.

A line is carelessly cast
in the sea's blue scrawl.
The tool bends its gentle crook
and turns its
eye upon itself.

The vessel groans and shifts,
restless in the waves,
as the line is sent,
grasping, into the dark.

Far below something awaits
and the line jumps with a start.
The sea whitens
in the moon's mad grip
and a man shivers on his boat.
The sea's grip tightens
and he disappears down its throat.


The Beginning of Night

Words are throbbing in the light.
Grinding gears are shaking,
rattling in the wind.
They're humming the lines
the keys are playing
as they chatter
in the cold.

Fog is billowing
as the light changes
and the fool dances
before his mic.

The light shatters
in its strobing,
the bits
falling in the crowd.
In the front row
a zealot rages,
grasping after the damaged
and twisted lights,
while, from the stage,
a laughter sounds
at this ending of days.


A Turning of Keys

Bars of light are sliding
through the trees.
Leaves are rippling
on the branch
like the turning of keys.

A car is writing
a tale of roads
in long, lazy curves.

Autumn bursts like a match,
sets the trees smoldering,
ash falling as the leaves burn.
The wind blows
a few puffs of smoke
into the last bit of sky.

A candle is sitting,
flickering,
in an orange sun,
smiling at the streets below.

The moon rides into the night.
You can almost hear those engines hum.


Broken

Clouds bubbled in the sky
as sunlight poured down
into the clear day.

Light laughter
like glasses arose
as teeth clinked.

In the sunlight
eyes rapidly blinked,
devouring the glare.

The weird play
of dancing trash
stumbled down the road
in a gust of air.

A face shattered,
wide open,
with a crack
and any bonds or vows
were neatly broken.


Turning On

The earth turns
its eye to the sun
as it spins its way
through the night.

A dervish's trail
unfurls
in sandy lines
and candles wink
at the sight.

A puff
of dust whirls
through the temple
of the night
and settles
in blinking signs
and eyefuls of sleep.


Ex Nihilo

From the dark abyss
a line
of light
erupts.
In the simple
hunger of emptiness
it grows serpentine,
bending itself,
coiling,
the glint
of stars
exploding on its back.

A spring lunges,
dark earth bursting apart
as it swells.

The needless
blossom is cracked.


The Patient Sky

The flower opens its eye,
lids unfurling,
and the fruit of the earth
drips onto the soil.

The liberation of decay
disintegrates its rind,
like great towers turning
to sand in the unending surge
of the patient sky,
with the thankless toil
and unceasing work
of bitter days.

The nights are growing festive
and the winter's flurries deep.

Ships are growing restless
as they bob in the distilling seas.

The winds are taken to screaming...
and to the sowing of seeds.


Passing Through

The night
swings its bat
at an orange sun
and it shatters
into twilight.
Seeds of light
are scattered
across the sky
as the darkness runs.

Behind its mask
a ghost pursues
the night
as the winds
pass through
the dark.

Torn from their husk
the stars fly free,
hurtling through space.

The ghost caresses its new face
and, though by night winds unmoved,
the garb
it wears is unwinding
like the fading of the mist
in the risen sun.


A Quiet Song

The sky waves
its banner,
it whips in the wind,
and light rain
sounds a march
across the damp earth.

With a spark
the storm's rapid fuse
is ablaze.
The daylight explodes
with a crash,
metal contorting
through the sky.

In the haze
twisted wreckage looms
and settles
in a slow collapse.

The storm seems
to give up,
loosen its tight grasp
on the rain
and the light.
The sound of its drums
has run thin.

A white flag
ripples in the sky
and is gone
leaving just the wind
blowing through
the clear blue
with its quiet song.


Lasting But a Moment...

The sun has fallen
and the day's funeral
has begun,
lowering its coffin
into the earth. The
sky grows dark
with mourning.

Wine sloshes into place
before the glasses have risen
in their toasting,
flashing with dull sparks
in the flickering light.

The crickets are singing
in the tittering night
and the stars do shake
with laughter.

Lights are winking
in the hereafter.


The Mapmaker

The sky writes
a cryptic scrawl
across the erg
in crescents of sand—
a cypher to peruse
found in the sickle
of the wind
and the remains
it leaves behind.

Like an iceberg
adrift on the deep
a ruin floats on grains
of dirt tossed about
by mercurial, fickle
winds in this arid land—
an abandoned keep
surrendered to the sky
in a sandy fall.

Dusty soldiers are lined
up on the stony front
as the sky fights
with its forces pinned
against a broken redoubt.

A squawk comes over the cable
inspiring a ruthless hunt
as the general turns his eye
to the maps on his table
in search of further clues.


Taming the Sky

The moon's white hand
rises into the sky.
The stars tremble
in a dying of the light.

Pale spiders skitter
across the skin of the night
before the first blush of dawn.
The jaundiced sky withers
at its bleeding horizon.

A song
rises inside the temple
from the mouth
of an eclipsed sun
and resounds
in the bright crash
of an echoing
gong.

A tamed lion's
roaring
settles down
in the mist of
not morning.


Frame

Clouds outline the morning,
tracing the body of the sky,
but cannot staunch
the surging dawn.

The stain
of light
spreads
but the
stars die
slowly.

The earth is placed
in a frame
of parched skies.

The shadowy tread
of night
steps on bones,
with the boots
of the
wondering mob.

The lost cogs
of a creaking
subterfuge
are rolling
across the sky
and its face
is bleeding.


The Guest

Crouching in its
disheveled yard,
it bows to the wind,
murmuring an obsequity
in creaking timber.

Wrinkles of
failing paint
marble its skin,
and fall apart:
a trickling
of stars,
as constellations
undo themselves.

Bricks pulled from
the foundation
lie beneath windows
that wait to be broken.
Wielding its bones,
a vandal will deface
the fragile skin,
now that it's thinner.

Its door swings open
in a polite gesture
of invitation
to the weather.

The rope lies broken
in the forgotten well.


The Sun Is Setting

Birds squawk
in a jagged dance
of sharpened angles
in a fog-filled marsh,
cloudy forms writhing
in a vague discourse.

The sewers whisper
to the streets
in a breath of steam
where the modern talk
of the kidnapped
is enslaved to vandals
in a scrawl of the dying.

This litter,
this hard
rain of confetti,
it forever falls
when the sun is setting.




II




The Beating of Wings

Just one small slip and
a green vine is shattering,
spraying drunken blood

across the witchgrass.
Provoked by this injury
a deep momentum

takes me by the hand,
grabs hold of the jagged leaves,
liberates my heart

from its dark crevasse.
Inside, the red baptistry
drips with the high art

of this sacrifice.
My heart, finally unstuck,
flutters with freedom

and begins to rise.
It beats its wings like a dove,
flapping only once.


Vacant Ground

Quickly starlight dust
vanishes in a white puff
of darkling magic.

As the plot blackens,
slowly a thread unravels
in the blind vandal's

blood. Crimson trolleys
clatter, sweeping the lost streets
until they're empty.

Vacant ground: dead seeds
shift, stir, awaken, and burst
in arid splendor.

And now a mad thirst
is trembling in the throat,
singing its one note,

ringing forever,
the sound of the unanswered
finally shattered.


Angle

Massing on the east edge
of the chair, a smokiness
flanks a battered cigar.
Ash falls like a thick scar
sliding from a glowing wound
when he but barely moves.

His chair offers a plea
and I hear something
collapse beyond the wall.

Ice drops into the alcohol,
a celestial body disappearing
into that small clear sea.
Pounding walls of melted sand,
it cracks its frozen hands
on an invisible prison.

My hands grip the glassy finish
of these two broken arms,
knotted in wooden scars
like little pine glyphs,
as I strangle the place I sit.

What has the keenness
to scar the flat cement
of the dried brick heart
of a man this parched?
What tortured sestina
can I throw between us?
What repetition can refute
the refrains of bleak truth
that a loaded gun speaks?


The Dark Fiber

A splash of white wings
ripples the blacktop river
as a scribble of dark water
crosses the stunted pages.

Fingers of black sewage
turn whispering pages
as the storm rocks
in its splashing chair.

The treadstone hisses
its rolling headlines
then suddenly swerves
to pick up the news
with splashing hands.

The paper rips apart
like an exploding star,
shattering under
the undercarriage,
as headlights flicker,

lighting up

the dark fiber

of the night sky.


A Celebration

...that's when they spoke
of the curse of the temple,
and of how the obscene
must lie forever unknown,
of the law of the kings.

The sun had fallen
and the workers fled,
and wordless scribes
had dropped their pens

The night was yellow
with the unsettled
dust of this blasphemy

The day's frail ghost
shakes all the bones
that it possesses
to lose the unforgotten
in a macabre procession.


Valet Parking

The valets are massing
as the night throws cars
in thin black lines
of calligrapher's art,
and they're sighing
across pages of night.

Like a pendant dancing
in her decolletage,
a new suit-and-tie
swings from her arm,
his teeth trying
to catch the light.

Random muses hatching
from the restaurant
crack the doors at nine.
Men signal for their cars
while valets are busy rifling
through the contents of the ride.


Hunger

The cars are slouching
to the city's jaundiced beat
of acrylic cuts.

The sweet rose's sound
accents the wafting tongues
of billboard preachers.

Here at the crossroads,
sour apples tempt the shrunken,
who no longer reach.

The lotus blossom
flickers open on the screen,
and it sings of lust.

The sleepwalkers step
into the mouths of their gowns,
mumbling bedtime tales.

Here, where the world ends,
unwounded dragons awake
the devourer.


Courtship

He serenades the sky,
in romantic despair,
and the wind is sighing.

When her gray beauty strides
across the blue barrens,
he serenades the sky.

He praises her unkindness
with his songs of swearing,
and the wind is sighing.

When she roars with violence
and her tears are harrowing,
he serenades the sky.

He watches the storm subside,
finds himself not caring,
and the wind is sighing.

When the winds fall silent,
he plays a mournful air.
He serenades the sky,
and the wind is sighing.


Drifting Away

My skin is rended
by the sun's convulsing light
as spring comes thrusting
forth with a rustling cry
and into my empty limbs.

As summer ascends,
red flames burn on my branches
with their drooling tongues
until they're full on my sap
and leave in a steady dripping.

Autumn is subtle,
and I am slowly bereaved
as it works in stealth,
leaves drifting off in the breeze
as they're seduced by the wind.

In long sobbing gusts,
my naked limbs are shaking
when winter descends
its delicate spiral case,
bringing my last visitor.




III




In the Orchard

The burning of stars flecks
the sky like spilled sugar,
the cold white light
as soft as a blanket.

There is white earth on the grave.

There are white sheets on the bed.

There is white snow in the cornfield.

A dark and tattered tree
sits by a crystalline brook
that laughs over the stones.

Autumn cries with its trees
and shakes in sobbing gusts.

The leaves rustle in the wind
like a sleeper shifting in bed.

Dressed in mourning black
I walk through the orchard
among the heavy gusts of leaves
in November's orange light,
which is like a warm apple cider,
waiting for the apples to bloom,
to blossom out of nothing
like a scream.


Ablaze

The autumn winds dance

Green icebergs drift

Dressed in black
I slide through
the storm of leaves
until its soft

rolling across
silver-gray rock
like a moon
directing the
warm waters.

The moon shines
over a black stretch
of forests covered

The bark is black

Burning in the
autumnal light,
the fields blaze
with Indian corn.


Darkness Waits

A black street curves

Peaches smolder
their cold light
across the asphalt.

A yellow candle burns
behind the dark pane

I put my palm
to the cool glass

at the brown table

A candle burns
in a red glass

Night spreads its deep blue

A black chandelier

Darkness waits

An old wooden gate
swings back and forth

A blind is closed
and then opened again.
The window is a square

A wash of light

In the dark distance

I am looking

Away, Into, Away

In the chill blue air
a purple flower
peels itself open,
stretching until
its petals fall away.

One of them spirals
through a vast,

until it's frozen
in a block of ice.

A naked woman in a blue room,

In the burning yellow air,

The wind is gentle

It's Time

The air is cool and still:
white streaks in blue marble.
Crickets are singing offstage.

A bush waves in greeting
with its dusty green hand
while it whispers something
in your ear.

A coarse path lies before you,
graying into the cold distance.
Your steps are hungry
and they crunch the miles,
with the mouth of the garden open.

A burgundy flower is there,
the thorns daring you
to pick up the glass,
to know the taste
you'll never have.

Sapphire burns in the sky;
sounds like a shattering,
tastes like glass: sweet.

Don't you think it's time
you closed your eyes?


Erosion

The clouds are sighing
and the wind is soaked
in the storm's flat grey.

The rain stumbles,
tripping on salted earth,
collapsing into silt seas.

The waves scribble
on the blank tides
with their blue pen
until the ink runs low.

The ground is erased
in whispering strokes
of grey debris.

The world's
demoralized breath
shuffles by,
carting the land away.




IV




Ghosts

In the speckled dark,
in the airless murk,
in my starless sight,
I can see the heat
of my body burn
as air is torn
from nothingness.

I walk a brisk pace
down nonexistent paths.
The bones of sloths
are a white pavement
which no ghosts haunt
because there is no past
to lay them to waste.


Parenthetical

—I have never retreated,
I have only advanced.—

(A victorious enemy
marches on my tongue.)

[My own private Vietnam
in every syllable I sing.]

(I taste mud with every tread
and every tastebud they gain.)

[Every breath is occupied
and lies in glowing ruin.]

(Their measured footfalls
grinding teeth in perfect time.)

[I'm burning syllables
in order to save them.]

(I can feel the jagged pang
of enamel beneath their boots.)

[Sibilants are writhing
in a hiss of bullets.]

(They reach my lips' wet halls,
soldiers slipping in the pink.)

[A guttural growls
under a purring tank.]

(I no longer hear the din
of the mouth they maim.)

[Vowels are held down
and made to scream.]

—I have walked so far
only to surrender.—


Red Rock

A wilted flower crumbles open,
entreating the beehive to sting,
to enliven with their dyeing pain
and with their murmuring song.

It wobbles at every touch,
in an uncertain dance,
sighing out of reach
in the weather's violence.

It moves in a windy dodge,
with an atmospheric scream.
Its whipped along the ridge
and then severed at the stem.

In the arroyo's crimson tear,
it feathers into the dust.
It will spring up again later
when the rains bring their unrest.


A Great Reward

The green leaves of fame
are cooling on my head.

—Blood shatters its violent gem
across a bed someone else made.—

Glasses ring in icy tribute
as water condenses on the skin.

—From disease's dizzying height
I take in a spinning scene.—

I drink the sparkling praise
and my mind starts reeling.

—Sobbing in sanguine cries,
my heart beats its ragged song.—

Glasses weep joyful rivulets
of condensation upon my hand.

—My heart reads in painful fits
the final verse it's underlined.—

The poem's beautiful refrain
is a line of trochaic applause.

—Death scrawls its fevered rune
in front of my dreaming eyes.—

My green pen's scribbling rush
signs one autograph before I dash.

The Difference

The house of deformity
looks so peaceful tonight
sitting in the darkness.
But those doors open onto
a horrifying realm of infinite

obscenity with a maelstrom
of boundless corruption
contorting in the center
in a howling slipstream
glowing with a wet sheen.

As I creak the doors open,
I feel the darkness deepen,
moving in a crimson slither
through my quickening blood.
Through the screaming ether

a familiar voice reverberates
the words of distorted rites
that sound ancient and foreign,
a voice I'm hear to murder.
Into my wrist, I carve a rune

of the most perfect uniformity,
and raise my flowing stigmata
far above my reeling head.
Before me, I see a face ashen
as it stares at its reflection.


Golden

Before you, an array
of glass tubes glinting,
lit by the strange glow
of this or that fluid.
The indescribable scent
of dangerous chemicals

rises from the machinery
to rest on your tongue
with the effect of glue
as you try to open wide,
finding your voice absent.
You can hear the calls

of glass, voices airy
as they're asphyxiating.
Their insides are aglow
with a thickening fluid,
one last bubbling descent
into the burning coals.


A Gentle Breeze Blows

For these delicate flowers
arranged in their meadows,
there's power in every breeze.

These unblinking followers
flicker like candelabras
swaying in their windy daze.

With every shift of wind
they unresistingly curve
into the shape of force.

They twist and they unwind
contorting something fierce
as they painfully carve

the newest demanded shape
from their breaking fibers
in a strange unravelling.

Such fine craftsmanship!
The shapes are enthralling
in the light of dimming embers.


Shade

A parasol twirls across the sun,
grinding the light under foot,
and now nothing can be seen.

As you cast your desperate orison
toward the gods of blind fate,
a parasol twirls across the sun.

This act of stellar arson
grows dark in the firefight,
and now nothing can be seen.

As terror drives you insane
and your thoughts are forfeit,
a parasol twirls across the sun.

Dying flames sigh in unison
in a long smokeless defeat
and now nothing can be seen.

After the sun's burning lesson,
you can hear the night refute.
A parasol twirls across the sun
and now nothing can be seen.


The Mark

His flesh, open like a mouth,
speaks a language crimson
with the words of sacrifice.
He is casting a dark malison
as he stares into a mirror.

In the circle stands a mammoth
figure with eyes that smolder
with lies. A smoking artifice
sneaks out of the trapdoor
of his voice. "It's your career

that's at stake," he says.
"Finish what you've started.
Now give your name to me
and enter into my servitude."
He couldn't stand to pause,

to endure this yearning. Seize
the day! Fame is in that fire!
And the real enemy here is time.
If I sign now, in one short year
I will be making art that pays!

Back and Forth

of a fluttering bird
flickers soundless

A dirty thing
of dull color
flies north
passed the guard.

Slowly the geese
lay their claim,

The Tower

Constellations of broken glass
cover the floor in dusty light.
The gaping holes of jagged sky
they came from offer a broken view
to the one curled up inside.

A slight chill holds the air
like a hand of melting ice.

Dirt hovers like a dust mote
in the artificial dusk
as it fuzzies up the shadow.

And the speaker will hide
behind the rotting chair
as the wind ransacks the place.

The barbarian is at the gate
and the weather is brisk.


Drought

The wind is blowing
through the empty spaces,
that it alone created,
with its mournful whisper.

Grains of sand rattle
